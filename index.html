About

I am an AI Researcher at Boeing and an outside collaborator in Causality Lab @ SNU. Previously, I received B.S. degree at Yonsei University majoring in Electrical and Electronic Engineering with a minor in Astronomy. I was also an exchange student at UCLA and was fortunate to work with Prof. B.S. Manjunath at UCSB. My primary research interests in AI are representation learning and generative models. I believe that the goal of artificial intelligence is to create systems that are logically interpretable, explainable, and reliable, which ultimately can contribute to the scientific discovery.

As a Computer(70%) Scientist(30%), I am interested in:

  • Disentangled/causal representation learning
  • Object-centric learning
  • Self-/unsupervised learning

To become a Computer(30%) Scientist(70%), I am always keen to put more effort into:

  • AI for science
  • Science for AI
  • Neuro-symbolic AI

I am always open to collaborate within diverse fields :

  • Bringing neuroscience perspective to machine learning
  • Drug discovery / Protein representation learning (biology)
  • Astronomy / Aerospace Engineering

I'm curretnly working on 2 research projects - one for Causal Representation Learning and one for Vision-Language Model benchmark. If you are curious about my ongoing project, please contact me through email
